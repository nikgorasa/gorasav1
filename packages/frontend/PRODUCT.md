# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Premium Indian travelers booking flights, hotels, and holiday packages; corporate travel desks managing bulk bookings and wallets; B2B agents sourcing wholesale GDS rates for their clients. They're on desktop or mobile, often multitasking between work and travel planning, expecting speed and trust over flash.

## Product Purpose

GoRASA is a luxury travel tech platform that connects Indian travelers with premium domestic and international flights, boutique hotel stays, and curated holiday packages without the usual OTA friction. Success means users feel the transaction is safe, the rates are live-verified, and a concierge is reachable within one tap.

## Brand Personality

Opulent, Trustworthy, Bold. The tone is confident but warm — a well-dressed concierge, not a pushy salesman. Luxury without pretension, technology without coldness.

## Anti-references

Not a clone of MakeMyTrip, Cleartrip, or any dense coupon-heavy OTA. No closeout-sale aesthetics, no blinking discount badges, no visual noise. Also not sterile fintech or generic SaaS-cream — travel needs warmth and aspiration.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through transparency.** Every price has a verified-rate lock, every booking has a PNR, every action has clear feedback. Surface the system state.
2. **Luxury is restraint.** Fewer elements, better spacing, intentional typography. White space signals premium, not emptiness.
3. **One tap to a human.** The WhatsApp concierge is never more than a click away. Design the interface around this safety net.
4. **Motion with purpose.** Animations explain transitions, never decorate. Micro-interactions confirm actions, not distract.
5. **Bilingual by nature.** English-first but swappable — every label in the future should work in Hindi and other Indian languages without layout breakage.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Touch targets ≥44px. All icons have text labels or aria-labels. Support screen readers for booking flows and PNR status. Color is never the sole differentiator. Respect reduced-motion preferences.
